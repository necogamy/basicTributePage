Bobby Fischer

Following the career of the Chess world champion

  • 1959 - In the heritage of the Candidates tournament, in Yugoslavia (it was played in three cities: Bled, Zagreb and Belgrade), he finished in fifth place, tied on points with Svetozar Gligorić, a great international chess figure; this time Fischer lost all four of his games to Tal.
  • 1960 - In Leipzig (Germany), he drew spectacularly with the Soviet then world champion Mikhail Tal.
  • 1970 - He obtained first place in the Palma de Mallorca Interzonal Tournament (Balearic Islands, Spain), in which he won fifteen of the twenty-four games he played (the last seven of the tournament consecutively), something truly unusual considering the level of tournament.
  • 1971 - In the Candidates final, Fischer defeated the former world champion, the Soviet Tigrán Petrosián, in Buenos Aires (Argentina) by 6.5 to 2.5, thereby earning the right to face Spasski with the world title at stake.
  • 1972 - The so-called "Encounter of the Century" it was a match between challenger Bobby Fischer of the United States and defending champion Boris Spassky of the Soviet Union. The match was played in Reykjavik, Iceland. Being the winner of this duel Bobby Fischer, earning the title of "World Chess Champion".
  • 1992 - Twenty years later, he played an exhibition match against his old rival Spasski.

Bobby Fischer won all the tournaments in which he participated from December 1962 to the 1972 World Championship, with only two exceptions: the 1965 Capablanca Memorial Tournament (held in Havana and Bobby played by teletype from Nueva York), in which he was tied for second with Borislav Ivkov and Geller, half a point behind winner Smyslov; and the 1966 Piatigorsky Cup, in which he ranked second, one and a half points behind Spasski.
